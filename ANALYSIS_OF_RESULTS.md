## 11. COMPARISON WITH TRADITIONAL SYSTEM

### 11.1 Data Collection Methodology

**Note on Data Usage:**
Due to ethical considerations and limitations outlined in the research methodology, this study utilized:
- **Simulated data** for system demonstration and functionality testing
- **Actual manual process data** collected through NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) from barangay offices
- **Comparative analysis** between traditional manual processes and the proposed digital system

The simulated data was used to demonstrate system capabilities while protecting the privacy of actual complainants. Actual manual process data (date filed, complaint type, resolution date) was collected under NDA to establish baseline metrics for comparison.

### 11.2 Manual Process Data Analysis

**Data Collected from Traditional System (Under NDA):**

**Sample Details:**
- **Total Complaints Analyzed**: 20 complaints
- **Data Sources**: 4 barangays in Davao City
- **Collection Period**: November 2024 - November 2025
- **Data Collection Method**: NDA agreements with barangay officials
- **Processing Method**: Paper-based, manual tracking

**NDA Document 1 (Barangay SLO):**

- Complaints: 5 cases
- Types: Cohesion & Community Issues (2), Illegal Physical Activity, Threats, Single Slander
- Resolution times: 4-14 days

**NDA Document 2 (Barangay SLO):**
- Date: November 2025  
- Complaints: 5 cases
- Types: Violation of Noise, Loud Disposal, Theft, Garbage Improper, Threat
- Resolution times: 1-31 days

**NDA Document 3 (Barangay SOCO):**
- Date: April 2025
- Complaints: 5 cases
- Types: Collection of Money, Loud Disposal, Physical Injury, Trespassing Property, Neighboring Quarrel
- Resolution times: 2-14 days

**NDA Document 4 (Barangay Bajada):**
- Date: August 2025
- Complaints: 5 cases
- Types: Physical Injury, Loud Verbal, Slander, Destroyed Item/Property, Property Damage
- Resolution times: 8-21 days

**Calculated Metrics:**
- **Average Resolution Time**: 10.8 days

- **Slowest Resolution**: 31 days (Theft - NDA Doc 2)
- **Median Resolution Time**: 9 days
- **Most Common Issues**: Noise complaints, Property disputes, Physical altercations

**Key Findings from Manual Data:**
- Average time from filing to resolution: **10.8 days**
- Complaint tracking method: Manual logbook/paper forms
- Communication method: Phone calls and physical visits to barangay office
- Status inquiry method: In-person inquiry at barangay office
- Record accessibility: Office hours only (8AM-5PM), physical files only
- Documentation: Handwritten forms, no digital backup
- Data retrieval: Manual search through paper files

### 12.2 Current Limitations

1. **Internet Dependency**
   - Requires stable internet connection
   - May be challenging in areas with poor connectivity
   - **Mitigation**: Mobile optimization for slower connections

2. **Digital Literacy**
   - Users need basic computer/smartphone skills
   - **Mitigation**: User-friendly interface design

3. **Emergency Cases**
   - System focuses on non-emergency complaints
   - **Mitigation**: Clear guidelines on emergency procedures

4. **Initial Setup**
   - Requires admin training and onboarding
   - **Mitigation**: Comprehensive documentation and support

5. **Data Collection Limitations (Ethical Considerations)**
   - **Limitation**: Cannot use actual citizen complaint data due to privacy concerns
   - **Approach**: Used simulated data for system demonstration
   - **Validation**: Collected anonymized manual process data under NDA for baseline comparison
   - **Justification**: Protects complainant privacy while demonstrating system capabilities
